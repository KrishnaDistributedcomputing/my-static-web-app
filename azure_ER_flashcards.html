What is Azure ExpressRoute?

Azure ExpressRoute is a service that provides a private connection between on-premises networks and Microsoft Azure datacenters, bypassing the public internet.

What are the key benefits of using Azure ExpressRoute?

The key benefits include lower latency, higher reliability, and faster speeds compared to typical internet connections.

What is ExpressRoute Direct?

ExpressRoute Direct allows you to connect directly into Microsoftâ€™s global network at peering locations strategically distributed across the world.

What is ExpressRoute Global Reach?

ExpressRoute Global Reach lets you connect your on-premises networks through the ExpressRoute locations across the world.

What are the monitoring and diagnostics features available in ExpressRoute?

Monitoring and diagnostics in ExpressRoute can be achieved through Azure Monitor and Network Performance Monitor to ensure network health and performance.

How does ExpressRoute ensure resiliency?

ExpressRoute ensures resiliency through multi-site and metro-area connections, providing diverse and redundant paths to Azure.

What security features does ExpressRoute offer?

ExpressRoute offers encryption options, including MACsec, and supports private connections that bypass the public internet, enhancing security.

What is the ExpressRoute Premium add-on?

The ExpressRoute Premium add-on provides increased route limits, global connectivity, and connectivity to Microsoft 365 services.

How does ExpressRoute handle redundancy?

ExpressRoute handles redundancy through active-active connections and multiple paths to ensure continuous availability.

What are the pricing models for ExpressRoute?

ExpressRoute pricing models include Unlimited Data and Metered Data plans, each with different cost structures based on data usage.

What SLA does ExpressRoute offer?

ExpressRoute offers a 99.95% SLA for circuit availability, ensuring high reliability for your connections.

What are the peering types available in ExpressRoute?

ExpressRoute supports Private Peering, Microsoft Peering, and Public Peering for different connectivity needs.

How is routing managed in ExpressRoute?

Routing in ExpressRoute is managed through BGP, allowing dynamic and efficient routing between on-premises and Azure.

How does Azure AD integrate with ExpressRoute?

Azure AD integrates with ExpressRoute to provide secure, seamless authentication and authorization for users accessing Azure services.

What types of connections are supported by ExpressRoute?

ExpressRoute supports any-to-any (IPVPN) networks, point-to-point Ethernet networks, and virtual cross-connections through a connectivity provider at a colocation facility.

What is the role of BGP in ExpressRoute?

BGP (Border Gateway Protocol) is used in ExpressRoute for dynamic routing between on-premises networks and Azure.

How does ExpressRoute provide encryption?

ExpressRoute provides encryption through MACsec for point-to-point encryption on ExpressRoute Direct circuits.

What are the bandwidth options available for ExpressRoute circuits?

The bandwidth options for ExpressRoute circuits include 50 Mbps, 100 Mbps, 200 Mbps, 500 Mbps, 1 Gbps, 2 Gbps, 5 Gbps, and 10 Gbps.

What is ExpressRoute FastPath?

ExpressRoute FastPath improves the data path performance between your on-premises network and your virtual network.

What connectivity models does ExpressRoute support?
